The Golden Rule of Crate Sizing

Your dog should be able to stand up without hitting their head, turn around easily, and lie down with legs extended. If buying for a puppy, get an adult-sized crate with a divider panel to adjust as they grow.

Crate Training Tips for Large Breeds

A crate is only useful if your dog accepts it as their space. Here's how to crate train a large breed effectively:

1. Make It Positive

Never use the crate as punishment. Feed meals in the crate, give high-value treats, and offer praise when they enter voluntarily. The crate should be the place where good things happen.

2. Start Slowly

Begin with 5-10 minute sessions while you're home. Gradually increase duration. Don't leave a puppy in the crate for more than their age in months plus one hour (3-month-old puppy = 4 hours max).

3. Exercise First

A tired dog is a crate-happy dog. Exercise your large breed before crate time so they're ready to rest, not ready to protest.

4. Comfortable Bedding

Add a crate mat or bed ‚Äî but only if your dog won't destroy it. Some dogs do better with just the plastic pan until they're past the chewing phase.

5. Ignore Whining (Within Reason)

If you let them out when they whine, they learn whining works. Wait for a quiet moment before opening the door. However, if whining is excessive or accompanied by distress signals, reassess your training approach.
