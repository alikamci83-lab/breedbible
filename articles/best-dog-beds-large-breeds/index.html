Best Dog Beds for Large Breeds

Orthopedic, Durable & Washable Picks ‚Ä¢ Updated February 2026

A quality dog bed isn't a luxury for large breeds ‚Äî it's essential. Golden Retrievers, Labradors, and German Shepherds put serious weight on their joints. A cheap, thin bed on a hard floor can contribute to arthritis, calluses, and poor sleep. The right bed supports their joints, regulates temperature, and gives them a space of their own.

In this guide, we review the best dog beds for large breeds, focusing on orthopedic support, durability, and ease of cleaning.

What to Look for in a Large Breed Dog Bed

  • Size: Your dog should be able to stretch out fully. For Goldens and Labs (55-80 lbs), look for beds 40"+ in length.
  • Orthopedic foam: Memory foam or egg-crate foam supports joints and prevents pressure points. Critical for seniors and breeds prone to hip dysplasia.
  • Bolster vs flat: Bolsters (raised edges) give dogs something to lean against. Flat beds offer more sprawling room. Know your dog's preference.
  • Removable, washable cover: Large dogs shed, drool, and get dirty. Machine-washable covers are non-negotiable.
  • Durability: Chew-resistant materials and reinforced stitching matter for heavy use.
  • Waterproof liner: Protects the foam from accidents and moisture.

The Bed Placement Rule

Place your dog's bed in a quiet, low-traffic area where they can still see family activity. Avoid drafty spots or direct sunlight. Many dogs prefer having two beds ‚Äî one in a social area (living room) and one in a quiet sleeping area (bedroom).

When to Replace a Dog Bed

  • Flattened foam: If you can feel the floor when pressing down, it's not providing support
  • Torn cover: Exposed foam becomes a chewing hazard and hygiene issue
  • Persistent odor: Even after washing, old beds harbor bacteria and smells
  • Your dog avoids it: If they choose the floor over their bed, something's wrong

General guideline: Replace orthopedic beds every 2-3 years, or sooner if you notice flattening. Elevated beds last longer (5+ years) if the fabric is intact.
